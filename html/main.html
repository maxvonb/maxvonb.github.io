Research Background

My PhD research focuses on characterizing the function of oscillatory protein dynamics in fission yeast cell polarization and growth. I use a combination of live-cell imaging, quantitative image analysis, and mathematical modeling to investigate how dynamic patterns of proteins contribute to cellular processes.

Career Vision

I'm passionate about interdisciplinary research at the intersection of biology, mathematics, and computer science. My goal is to leverage computational methods and quantitative analysis to uncover fundamental biological principles and contribute to advancements in fundamental biological research.
